Hyperion Industries

role: Media Director
tools: InDesign, Illustrator, Premiere, Lightroom
duration: September 2016 - June 2018

In my senior year of high school, I served as the media director of a mock engineering firm known as Hyperion Industries. Our task was to build a robot known was Shere Khan that would have the ability to run, jump, climb, and attach modules to itself.

My Role

As media director, my responsibilities were very diverse and included branding, graphic design, document design, video editing, and photography. My role in crafting the company's presentation was critical for fundraising; we fund-raised over $5000 to build the robot. Throughout the course of the school-year, I delivered brochures, Powerpoints, ID cards, video ads, water bottle labels, 8-foot high banners, designs for two 100-page documents, and a documentary.

Branding

Since the company was new, I first had to create the company brand. I informally surveyed members of the company asking them, "What do you want people to think when they hear our company?" Most responses included:

  • High-tech
  • Innovative
  • Modern
  • Bold

and other adjectives along those lines. With this information, I researched logos and design guidelines of companies such as Boeing, Northrop-Grumman, and Tesla, companies that team members associated with the adjectives above (we had a lot of future engineers).
